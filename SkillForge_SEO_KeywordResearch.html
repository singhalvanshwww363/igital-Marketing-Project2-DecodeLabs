The 4 Archetypes of Search Intent

Every keyword has a "why" behind it. Matching content to the correct intent separates page 1 from page 10.

📖 Informational

User is seeking knowledge. They want to learn.

e.g. "What is an API?" · "How does ML work?"

Content: Blog posts, guides, tutorials

🧭 Navigational

User is looking for a specific page or website.

e.g. "DecodeLabs Login" · "SkillForge dashboard"

Content: Homepage, landing pages

⚖️ Commercial

User is comparing options before deciding.

e.g. "Best Python Course 2026" · "SkillForge vs Internshala"

Content: Comparison articles, reviews

💳 Transactional ⭐ Priority

User is ready to act. Highest conversion potential.

e.g. "Apply for Data Science internship" · "Buy Python course"

Content: Product/service pages, CTAs

💡 SkillForge Priority: 60% Transactional + Commercial keywords. These drive enrollments. Informational content builds long-term authority.

Search Command Validation. Counts pages targeting a keyword in title.

Alphabet Soup Method

Type your seed keyword + each letter A-Z in Google search to find real queries people search.

Example: "data science internship a..." → autocomplete shows "for absolute beginners", "after 12th", "application process"

Why it works: Google's autocomplete = driven by real search volume. If it appears, people are actively searching it.

allintitle: Operator

In Google, type: allintitle: remote data science internship freshers 2026

This shows how many pages have that phrase in their title tag — your competition count for KGR calculation.

KGR — The Math of Least Resistance

KGR tells you exactly how competitive a keyword is before you invest content creation time into it.

The Formula

KGR = allintitle results ÷ monthly search volume
< 0.25
GREAT — Rank in days
0.25–1.00
MODERATE — May rank
> 1.00
BAD — Too competitive

Sample KGR Calculation

Keyword: "remote data science internship for freshers 2026"

Step 1: Google → allintitle: remote data science internship for freshers 2026

Step 2: Result = 420 pages with this in their title

Step 3: Monthly volume from Keyword Planner = 1,900

Step 4: KGR = 420 ÷ 1,900 = 0.22 ✓ GREAT

Conclusion: SkillForge can rank for this keyword within days of publishing optimized content.

← Title Tag (Highest Weight)  |  URL Slug  |  Meta Description (Drives Clicks) →
  • Title Tag: Primary keyword in first 60 characters. Highest SEO weight.
  • URL Slug: Short, keyword-rich. No stop words. e.g. /data-science-internship
  • Meta Description: 150–160 characters. Contains keyword. Drives CTR, not ranking directly.
  • H1 Tag: One per page. Must match primary keyword intent.
  • Body Content: Keyword density 1–2%. Use LSI keywords naturally throughout.

Execution Workflow — 5 Steps

The exact process used to research all 20 keywords in this project. Repeatable and scalable for any brand.
1

BRAINSTORM — Seed Keywords

Start with 5–10 broad topic ideas: "data science internship", "tech internship India", "Python course", "AICTE program". These are seeds — not final keywords.

2

EXPAND — Autocomplete & Variations

Use Google Autocomplete (Alphabet Soup Method) and Ubersuggest to expand each seed into 10–15 long-tail variations. Focus on 4+ word phrases for lower competition.

3

VALIDATE — Check Volume

Use Google Keyword Planner to verify monthly volume. Filter out below 200/month (too niche) and above 10,000 (too competitive for new sites).

4

CALCULATE — KGR Score

Run allintitle: search for each keyword. Divide by monthly volume. Keep only KGR < 0.25. These are your golden opportunities.

5

CATEGORIZE — Assign Search Intent

Label each keyword as Informational, Navigational, Commercial, or Transactional. Map to the correct content type (blog, landing page, comparison, product page).

🔓 Gatekeeper Rule: Completing this workflow = verified demand data. This unlocks Project 3: Paid Ads. Never spend ad budget without SEO-verified demand.
